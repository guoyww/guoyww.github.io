We propose Long Context Tuning (LCT) for scene-level video generation, to bridge the gap between current single-shot generation capability and real-world narrative video (e.g., movies) productions. In this scenario, a scene is a series of single-shot videos capturing coherent events unfolding over time with semantic and temporal consistency.

Architecture Designs for Long Context Tuning.

We solve the problem with a data-driven apporach by directly learning scene-level consistency upon data, and we minimize architectural inductive bias that may compromise scability. The core of our method is expanding the span of MMDiT's context window from single-shot to the entire scene. This modification is compatible with MMDiT-based single-shot video diffusion models and introduce no additional parameters, thus can be viewed as an following training stage after single-shot video generation pre-training.

We employ an interleaved 3D Rotary Position Embedding (RoPE) to encode scene-level video order, as well as an asyncronized diffusion timestep strategy to unify visual conditioning and diffusion samples. We further explore a context-causal architecture that enable efficient auto-regressive shot generation with KV-cache. Our experiments are conducted with an internal video model with 3B parameter scale. See our Research Paper for more details.

Notably, besides its superior scene-level video generation capability, we find Long Context Tuning enables several emerging model capability, including single-/multi-shot extension and compositional generation.

Interactively Direct a Multi-shot Story

Our model enables interactive multi-shot development. This facilitates an iterative production workflow where directors can progressively shape content shot-by-shot based on previously generated footage, eliminating the need for comprehensive upfront prompting and allowing for creative decision-making with immediate visual feedback.

Composable Generation

Remarkably, despite no explicit training for this capability, our model enables composable generation by accepting separate identity and environment images to synthesize coherent videos integrating these distinct elements. This ability emerges from the model's learned scene-level visual relations in the training corpus, where scenes frequently contain establishing environmental shots, character close-ups, and integrated shots depicting character-environment interactions.
