Abstract

We present the first method capable of photorealistically reconstructing a non-rigidly deforming scene using photos captured casually from mobile phones.

Our approach augments neural radiance fields (NeRF) by optimizing an additional continuous volumetric deformation field that maps each observed point into a canonical 3D NeRF. We observe that explicitly constraining these deformations to be locally rigid produces higher-quality results. We also regularize the deformation field with a novel elastic regularization that assists gradient-based optimization methods for coordinate-based models that often get stuck in local minima. By adapting principles from geometry processing and physical simulation to neural field-like models, we demonstrate the first approach capable of producing photorealistic free-viewpoint renderings of non-rigidly deforming scenes.

We show that Nerfies can turn casually captured selfie photos and videos into deformable NeRF models for photorealistic renderings of the subject from arbitrary viewing viewpoints, which we call "nerfies". We evaluate our method by collecting data using a rig with two mobile phones that take time-synchronized photos, yielding multiview images of the same pose at different viewpoints. We show that our method faithfully reconstructs non-rigidly deforming scenes and reproduces unseen views with high fidelity.
